1. Who We Are

Phygital is a SaaS platform that enables brands to create Digital Product Passports (DPP), NFC-linked product experiences, and consumer engagement campaigns. The platform is operated by Alessandro De Vito (data controller).

3. Data We Do Not Collect

  • We do not send wallet addresses to Resend (email provider)
  • We do not use third-party advertising trackers or cookies for marketing
  • We do not collect biometric data

7. Your Rights (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, you have the following rights:

  • Access (Art. 15): Request a copy of your personal data
  • Rectification (Art. 16): Correct inaccurate data
  • Erasure (Art. 17): Request deletion of your data
  • Portability (Art. 20): Receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Objection (Art. 21): Object to processing based on legitimate interest
  • Withdraw consent: At any time, without affecting prior processing

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@phygital.io. We will respond within 30 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

8. Security

We implement technical and organisational measures to protect your data, including: TLS encryption in transit, PostgreSQL encryption at rest, role-based access control, multi-factor authentication for all team members, and a comprehensive audit trail. See our Security Policy for details.

9. Cookies

The Phygital dashboard uses only technically necessary session cookies (Supabase Auth). No advertising or analytics cookies are set. No consent banner is required for technically necessary cookies under ePrivacy Directive recital 25.

10. Government and Law Enforcement Access

We disclose personal data to government authorities or law enforcement only when we are legally required to do so (GDPR Art. 6(1)(c) — legal obligation). We do not voluntarily provide personal data to any government body without a valid legal requirement.

When we receive a request, we will:

  • Verify its legal validity before complying
  • Disclose only the minimum data required by the specific legal obligation
  • Where legally permitted, notify the affected user or brand before complying
  • Where legally permitted, challenge requests that we believe to be overly broad or unlawful

We will notify affected users or brands of any legally permissible government request unless we are prohibited from doing so by a court order or applicable law. We publish transparency information about such requests in our annual security review.

11. Changes to This Policy

We will notify brand users by email (via Resend) of any material changes at least 14 days before they take effect. The version history is available in the commit history of this file.
